Personal Guide on Creating Color Pallete

12 Q1 ‘18

If there’s a bad habit on my early career as a designer is by picking a color palette that I saw from other great designers or look at similar industries and see how what might be the cool I could use for my current project and I didn’t spend time to articulate why I chose it. Most of the time using your instinct would work when choosing color but wouldn’t be nice if you put some thoughts why you picked a set of color that would work best to your audience.

So what are the goods in crafting your color pallete?

  1. To improve visual hierarchy
  2. To better communicate your message
  3. To make the design consistent
  4. To deliver the emotion you want user to feel see and feel.
  5. Save production cost (Page 14, Color Works by Eddie Opara)
